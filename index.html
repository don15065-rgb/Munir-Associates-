The Firm

A practice built on preparation.

We advise companies, financial institutions and individuals on contested matters before the courts, and on the commercial and regulatory questions that arise long before a dispute ever reaches them.

The firm appears across the civil, commercial, banking, customs and constitutional jurisdictions, and supports that court work with the drafting, advisory and compliance capability that regulated business now demands. Our clients range from public-sector institutions and banks to manufacturers, distributors and listed groups, in Pakistan and abroad.

We keep the bench deliberately small. The lawyer who takes instructions on a matter is, as a rule, the lawyer who argues it. That is a choice about quality rather than scale, and it is the reason clients return to us with the work that matters most to them.

Our Approach

Four habits we do not negotiate.

I

Preparation over performance

Most matters are decided in the file long before they are argued in the hall. We over-prepare as a matter of habit, because it is the cheapest advantage a client can be given.

II

Candour about prospects

A client is owed an honest reading of a matter before anything else, including the reading they did not want to hear. We give it early, and in writing where it helps.

III

One team, end to end

The lawyer who takes your instructions is the lawyer who argues your case. We keep the bench small so that nothing is mislaid in a handover.

IV

Discretion as standard

Much of our work touches a client's balance sheet, its brands or its standing. We treat all three with the care we would give our own.

Sector Focus

Pharmaceutical & life sciences.

Pharmaceutical and healthcare businesses operate inside one of the most heavily regulated and brand-sensitive sectors in the country. Their value sits in the integrity of their marks, the enforceability of their supply and distribution arrangements, and their standing with regulators. We advise across that full range, from the registration and defence of trademark portfolios to the contracts that move product through the market, and the disputes that occasionally follow.

i

Trademark portfolios

Registration, opposition, renewal and assignment of pharmaceutical marks, and the management of multi-brand portfolios held across affiliated and group entities.

ii

Brand protection

Action against counterfeit, passing-off and infringing products, including urgent injunctive relief and the litigation that secures it.

iii

Commercial & supply contracts

Manufacturing, distribution, supply, licensing, confidentiality and technology-transfer agreements with domestic and international counterparties.

iv

Regulatory & compliance

Advisory on regulatory obligations, anti-money-laundering and financial-crime controls, and corporate governance for groups and their affiliates.

v

Litigation & recovery

Contractual and commercial disputes, debt recovery, and proceedings before the civil, banking and High Courts.

vi

Corporate advisory

Governance, structuring and the routine corporate documentation that keeps a pharmaceutical group's affairs in order.

Our lawyers have managed pharmaceutical trademark portfolios and commercial documentation from inside the industry as well as in private practice, including for a major Pakistani pharmaceutical group and its affiliated manufacturing and laboratory companies.

Pro Bono

Access to the courts.

The courts belong to everyone, but in practice they are easiest to reach for those who can pay. We set aside a portion of the practice for people who cannot. If you have a genuine legal grievance and no realistic means of engaging counsel, you are welcome to write to us.

Pro bono matters receive the same preparation, the same candour about prospects, and the same standard of advocacy as any paying brief. The only difference is the fee note.

Whom we try to help · subject to availability
  • Persons with disabilities facing barriers in courts and public institutions
  • Widows, the elderly and others at risk of losing their homes or property
  • Low-income tenants and workers in genuine need of representation
  • Matters raising access-to-justice or public-interest questions
  • Persons from marginalised and underrepresented communities
